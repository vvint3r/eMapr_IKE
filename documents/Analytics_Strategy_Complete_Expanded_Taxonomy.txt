Level 1 - Initial: Ad hoc processes, limited analytics, siloed data
Level 2 - Developing: Basic processes, descriptive analytics, some integration
Level 3 - Defined: Standardized processes, diagnostic analytics, integrated systems
Level 4 - Managed: Optimized processes, predictive analytics, advanced capabilities
Level 5 - Optimizing: AI/ML integration, prescriptive analytics, continuous innovation

FRAMEWORK 3: ANALYTICS OPERATING MODEL DECISION MATRIX

Model Types vs. Organization Characteristics:

Centralized Model:
- Best for: Small-medium orgs, limited resources, need for consistency
- Strengths: Expertise concentration, standard practices, efficiency
- Challenges: Potential bottlenecks, distance from business

Decentralized Model:
- Best for: Large orgs, diverse business units, local autonomy
- Strengths: Business alignment, agility, domain expertise
- Challenges: Duplication, inconsistency, resource inefficiency

Hub & Spoke Model:
- Best for: Growing orgs, balance of centralization/decentralization
- Strengths: Standards + flexibility, scalability
- Challenges: Coordination complexity, role clarity

Center of Excellence Model:
- Best for: Mature orgs, innovation focus, best practice sharing
- Strengths: Innovation, knowledge sharing, capability building
- Challenges: Limited execution capacity, dependency on others
