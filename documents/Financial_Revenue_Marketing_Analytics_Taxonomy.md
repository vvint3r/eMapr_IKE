## 1. CORE FINANCIAL METRICS

### 1.1 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - Total Sales & Marketing Spend / Number of New Customers
  - Time Period Considerations (Monthly, Quarterly, Annual)
  - What to Include in CAC
    - Advertising Spend
    - Marketing Team Salaries
    - Sales Team Costs
    - Marketing Software & Tools
    - Agency Fees
    - Creative & Content Production
    - Events & Conferences
- **Types of CAC**
  - Average CAC
  - Incremental (Marginal) CAC
  - CAC by Channel
  - CAC by Cohort
  - CAC by Customer Segment
  - CAC by Geography
  - Blended CAC
- **CAC Analysis**
  - CAC Trends Over Time
  - CAC by Funnel Stage
  - CAC Benchmarking
  - CAC Payback Period
  - CAC vs. Industry Standards
- **CAC Optimization**
  - Channel Mix Optimization
  - Funnel Leak Identification
  - Conversion Rate Optimization
  - Targeting Refinement
  - Creative Testing
  - Landing Page Optimization

### 1.2 Customer Lifetime Value (LTV/CLV/CLTV)
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - LTV = Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) × Customer Lifetime
  - LTV = Customer Value × Average Customer Lifespan
  - Time Horizon Considerations
    - 1-Year LTV
    - 3-Year LTV
    - Infinite Time Horizon LTV
- **LTV Components**
  - Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
    - Average Purchase Value
    - Average Purchase Frequency
  - Customer Lifetime (LT)
    - Average Customer Lifespan
    - Churn Rate
    - Retention Rate
  - Gross Margin
  - Discount Rate (for NPV calculations)
- **Types of LTV**
  - Historic LTV
  - Predictive LTV
  - Recurring LTV (Subscription Models)
  - Transactional LTV (E-commerce)
  - LTV by Cohort
  - LTV by Channel
  - LTV by Customer Segment
- **LTV vs. Revenue vs. Profit**
  - Gross Merchandise Value (GMV)
  - Revenue (Top Line)
  - Contribution Profit
  - Net Profit
  - Profit-Based LTV (Preferred)
- **LTV Analysis**
  - LTV Growth Rate
  - LTV by Acquisition Source
  - LTV Distribution
  - LTV Segmentation
  - LTV Cohort Analysis
- **LTV Optimization**
  - Retention Strategy
  - Upselling & Cross-Selling
  - Customer Experience Improvement
  - Product Value Enhancement
  - Loyalty Programs
  - Churn Reduction

### 1.3 LTV:CAC Ratio
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - LTV:CAC = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost
  - Interpretation Guidelines
    - 1:1 = Breaking Even (Problem)
    - 2:1 = Concerning (Need Improvement)
    - 3:1 = Ideal/Healthy
    - 4:1+ = Potential Underinvestment
- **LTV:CAC by Segment**
  - By Channel
  - By Customer Type
  - By Geography
  - By Product Line
  - By Cohort
  - Pareto Analysis (80/20)
- **Applications**
  - Investment Decisions
  - Budget Allocation
  - Channel Prioritization
  - Investor Relations
  - Growth Sustainability
  - Market Expansion Evaluation
- **Optimization Strategies**
  - Increasing LTV (Retention, Upsell)
  - Decreasing CAC (Efficiency)
  - Balanced Approach
  - Segment-Specific Strategies

### 1.4 Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend
  - ROAS = Attributed Sales / Advertising Costs
  - Short-Term vs. Long-Term ROAS
- **Types of ROAS**
  - Platform ROAS (Ad Platform Reported)
  - True ROAS (Verified Performance)
  - First-Order ROAS
  - LTV-Based ROAS
  - Blended ROAS
  - Marginal ROAS
  - Incremental ROAS
- **ROAS by Context**
  - Campaign ROAS
  - Channel ROAS
  - Creative ROAS
  - Audience ROAS
  - Geographic ROAS
  - Device ROAS
  - Time Period ROAS
- **ROAS vs. Other Metrics**
  - ROAS vs. ROI
  - ROAS vs. CAC
  - ROAS vs. MER
  - Short-Term ROAS vs. LTV-Based ROAS
- **ROAS Optimization**
  - Budget Allocation
  - Bid Strategy Optimization
  - Creative Testing
  - Audience Refinement
  - Landing Page Optimization
  - Funnel Optimization
- **ROAS Limitations**
  - Doesn't Account for CAC
  - Platform Attribution Bias
  - Short-Term Focus
  - Revenue vs. Profit Confusion
  - Attribution Challenges

### 1.5 Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER)
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - MER = Total Revenue / Total Marketing Spend
  - Time Period: Typically Annual
  - Holistic View vs. ROAS
- **MER Components**
  - Total Revenue (All Sources)
  - Total Marketing Expenses
    - Advertising Costs
    - Marketing Team Salaries
    - Agencies & Consultants
    - Marketing Technology
    - Content Production
    - Events & Sponsorships
- **MER vs. ROAS**
  - Broader Scope (All Marketing)
  - Includes Brand & Awareness Activities
  - More Strategic Metric
  - Harder to Calculate
  - Better for Overall Efficiency
- **MER Analysis**
  - MER Trends Over Time
  - MER Benchmarking
  - MER by Business Unit
  - MER Target Setting
- **MER Guidelines**
  - 3:1 = Minimum Viable
  - 4:1 = Good Performance
  - 5:1+ = Excellent Performance
  - Industry Variations

### 1.6 Return on Investment (ROI)
- **Definition & Calculation**
  - ROI = (Revenue - Investment) / Investment
  - ROI = (Profit from Marketing / Marketing Investment) × 100%
  - Marketing ROI (MROI/ROMI)
- **Types of ROI**
  - Campaign ROI
  - Channel ROI
  - Program ROI
  - Overall Marketing ROI
  - Incremental ROI
  - Marginal ROI
- **ROI Components**
  - Incremental Revenue
  - Incremental Contribution
  - Investment/Spend
  - Attribution to Marketing
- **ROI Analysis**
  - ROI by Channel
  - ROI by Campaign
  - ROI Trends
  - ROI vs. Benchmarks
  - ROI Sensitivity Analysis
- **Advanced ROI Concepts**
  - Media Inflation Impact
  - Diminishing Returns
  - Saturation Effects
  - Adstock Effects (Lagged Impact)
  - Halo Effects
  - Synergy Effects

### 1.7 Unit Economics
- **Core Metrics**
  - Average Order Value (AOV)
  - Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)
  - Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA)
  - Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
  - Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

### 1.8 Conversion & Engagement Metrics

  - Conversion Rate (CR)

## 3. ATTRIBUTION MODELING

### 3.1 Attribution Fundamentals
- **Definition & Purpose**
  - Assigning Credit to Touchpoints
  - Understanding Customer Journey
  - Optimizing Marketing Mix
  - Budget Allocation
  - Channel Performance
- **Attribution Challenges**
  - Multi-Touch Complexity
  - Cross-Device Tracking
  - Privacy Limitations (Cookies, GDPR)
  - Offline-Online Integration
  - Dark Social
  - Assisted Conversions

### 3.2 Single-Touch Attribution Models
- **Last-Touch (Last-Click) Attribution**
  - Definition: All credit to last interaction
  - Pros: Simple, easy to implement
  - Cons: Ignores journey, over-credits bottom funnel
  - Use Cases: Short sales cycles, direct response
- **First-Touch (First-Click) Attribution**
  - Definition: All credit to first interaction
  - Pros: Values awareness, acquisition focus
  - Cons: Ignores nurture, over-credits top funnel
  - Use Cases: Brand awareness focus, lead gen
- **Last Non-Direct Click**
  - Definition: Credit to last touchpoint excluding direct
  - Pros: Filters out branded search bias
  - Cons: Still single-touch limitations

### 3.3 Multi-Touch Attribution Models
- **Linear Attribution**
  - Equal credit to all touchpoints
  - Pros: Simple, recognizes all touches
  - Cons: No differentiation, all equal weight
  - Use Cases: Consistent brand messaging
- **Time-Decay Attribution**
  - More weight to recent touchpoints
  - Exponential decay function
  - Pros: Values recency, logical for decisions
  - Cons: May undervalue awareness
  - Use Cases: B2B, longer sales cycles
- **Position-Based (U-Shaped) Attribution**
  - 40% first touch, 40% last touch, 20% middle
  - Pros: Values discovery and close
  - Cons: Arbitrary weightings
  - Use Cases: Balanced approach
- **W-Shaped Attribution**
  - Credit to first, middle milestone, last
  - Common for lead-based models
  - Pros: Recognizes key milestones
  - Cons: Complex, needs clear milestones
- **Custom Rule-Based Attribution**
  - Business-defined rules and weights
  - Pros: Tailored to business model
  - Cons: Subjective, requires expertise

### 3.4 Data-Driven Attribution (Algorithmic)
- **Machine Learning Attribution**
  - Statistical Analysis of Paths
  - Dynamic Credit Allocation
  - Probabilistic Models
  - Path Analysis Algorithms
- **Data-Driven Attribution Benefits**
  - Removes Bias
  - Adapts to Data
  - More Accurate
  - Path-Level Insights
- **Requirements**
  - Sufficient Conversion Volume
  - Complete Path Data
  - Advanced Analytics Capability
  - Computational Resources
- **Platforms with Data-Driven Attribution**
  - Google Analytics 4
  - Adobe Analytics
  - Facebook Attribution (Deprecated)
  - Third-Party Platforms (Northbeam, Rockerbox, Triple Whale)

### 3.5 Advanced Attribution Concepts
- **Incrementality Testing**
  - Geo-Testing

  - Randomized Controlled Trials (RCT)
  - True Causal Measurement
- **Shapley Value Attribution**
  - Game Theory Application

### 5.2 Budget Allocation Models
- **Percentage of Revenue**
  - Fixed % of Projected Revenue
  - Typical: 5-15% for B2C, 2-10% for B2B
  - Scales with Business Size
  - Simple & Predictable
  - Pros: Easy, Scalable
  - Cons: May not align with growth needs
- **Objective-Based (Task-Oriented)**
  - Bottom-Up Approach
  - List Required Initiatives
  - Estimate Costs per Initiative
  - Sum = Total Budget
  - Pros: Detailed, Goal-Aligned
  - Cons: Time-Consuming, May Miss Big Picture
- **Competitive Parity**
  - Match Competitor Spending
  - Share of Voice Strategy
  - Pros: Maintains Competitiveness
  - Cons: Doesn't Guarantee Efficiency
- **Affordable Method**
  - What's Left After Other Expenses
  - Cons: Reactive, Not Strategic
  - Use: Only for Small/Starting Businesses
- **Zero-Based Budgeting**
  - Start from Zero Each Period
  - Justify Every Dollar
  - Pros: Eliminates Waste
  - Cons: Time-Intensive
- **Historical (Incremental)**
  - Previous Year + % Increase
  - Simple & Quick
  - Cons: Perpetuates Past Mistakes
- **Data-Driven Allocation**
  - MMM-Based Allocation
  - Attribution-Based Allocation
  - ROI-Optimized Allocation
  - Machine Learning Optimization

## 6. PRICING STRATEGY & ELASTICITY

### 6.1 Price Elasticity Fundamentals
- **Definition**
  - Price Elasticity of Demand (PED)
  - Relationship Between Price & Demand
  - Sensitivity to Price Changes
- **Calculation**
  - PED = % Change in Quantity Demanded / % Change in Price
  - Elasticity Coefficient Interpretation
- **Elasticity Types**
  - Elastic Demand (PED > 1)
    - Price-Sensitive Customers
    - Price Decrease → Large Demand Increase
  - Inelastic Demand (PED < 1)
    - Price-Insensitive Customers
    - Price Increase → Small Demand Decrease
  - Unit Elastic (PED = 1)
    - Proportional Response
  - Perfectly Elastic (PED = ∞)
  - Perfectly Inelastic (PED = 0)

### 6.2 Factors Affecting Price Elasticity
